2025 marked an unprecedented year in Hong Kong's tropical cyclone warning history:

  • First Double Signal 10 Year Since 1964: Wipha (July) and Ragasa (September) both triggered Hurricane Signal No. 10, marking the first time two Signal 10 warnings were issued in a single year since Typhoons Ruby and Dot in 1964
  • Record-Breaking Distance for Signal 10 (Ragasa): At 120 km from Hong Kong, Ragasa became the furthest typhoon to ever trigger Signal 10, surpassing the previous record of 100 km held jointly by Typhoon Mangkhut (2018) and Typhoon Vicente (2012)
  • Second-Longest Signal 10 Duration (Ragasa): The Signal 10 warning lasted 10 hours 40 minutes, the second-longest duration on record after Tropical Storm York's 11 hours in 1999
  • Late-Season Extremes (Toraji): Toraji (November 13-14, 2024) represented the latest Signal 8 issuance of the year in recent decades, highlighting extended typhoon season activity

These milestones underscore Hong Kong's continued vulnerability to severe tropical cyclones and the critical importance of HKO's forecast-based early warning system in protecting public safety.

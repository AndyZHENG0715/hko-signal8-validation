About This Project

Independent validation for transparency and public understanding

üéØ Project Purpose

This project provides independent, data-driven validation of Hong Kong Observatory's (HKO) Signal No. 8 issuance timing during tropical cyclone events. Our goal is to enhance transparency and public understanding of how typhoon warnings work, not to criticize HKO operations.

By comparing official signal periods with observation-based detection algorithms, we demonstrate the value of forecast-based early warning systems and validate that HKO follows published standards appropriately.

üî¨ Methodology

We analyze HKO's publicly available 10-minute mean wind speed data from 8 reference stations during typhoon events. Our algorithm applies simple criteria: Signal 8 conditions are detected when ‚â•4 stations simultaneously report sustained winds ‚â•63 km/h for ‚â•2 consecutive 10-minute periods.

This retrospective, observation-only approach intentionally differs from HKO's operational forecast-based system to highlight the value of early warning lead time.

‚öñÔ∏è Limitations

  • Our algorithm does NOT incorporate forecast data, cyclone track predictions, or nowcasting
  • We exclude high-ground and offshore stations that HKO considers for operational decisions
  • Eye passage continuity is not modeled, causing detection failures for direct-hit typhoons
  • This is validation research only‚Äînot a forecasting or real-time warning system

‚úÖ Key Findings

  • HKO's Signal 8 issuances are appropriate and consistent with published standards
  • Timing differences reflect valuable forecast-based early warning lead time (avg. 330 min for Talim 2023)
  • Non-detection in some events (Tapah, Toraji) consistent with localized/offshore wind patterns
  • Algorithm failures (Wipha eye passage) highlight limitations of simple observation-only logic

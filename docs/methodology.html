Understanding the Science

How typhoon signals are issued and how our validation works

HKO OFFICIAL PROCESS

Forecast-based early warning

1️⃣

Typhoon Track Forecast

Predict typhoon path and intensity 12-24 hours ahead using numerical models and satellite data

2️⃣

Wind Speed Prediction

Expected sustained winds ≥63 km/h at reference stations based on forecast track and intensity

3️⃣

Reference Station Check

Expect ≥4 of 8 reference stations to reach gale-force winds, considering exposure and terrain

4️⃣

Signal 8 Issued

Public warning issued with lead time for preparation: secure property, cancel activities, seek shelter

5️⃣

Monitor & Update

Continuous monitoring and signal updates as conditions change; cancel when gales no longer expected

OUR ALGORITHM

Observation-only validation

1️⃣

Observe Actual Wind Readings

Real-time 10-minute mean wind speeds collected every 10 minutes from all reference stations

2️⃣

Count Stations ≥63 km/h

Check how many of 8 reference stations currently experiencing sustained gale-force winds (observed data only)

3️⃣

Check Persistence

Require ≥4 stations ≥63 km/h for ≥2 consecutive 10-min periods to filter out transient spikes

4️⃣

Retrospective Validation

Compare algorithm detection timing with official signal periods; measure lead time and coverage

5️⃣

Generate Report

Create validation report with timing deltas, station summaries, and assessment of appropriateness

KEY DIFFERENCE: Forecast vs. Observation

HKO issues signals based on what is expected to happen (forecast), while our algorithm validates using what actually happened (observation). This fundamental difference explains timing gaps and is intentional for public safety.

Frequently Asked Questions

❓ Why is the algorithm always later?

Because HKO uses forecast data while our algorithm only validates using observed data. The time difference represents valuable early warning lead time, not premature issuance.

❓ Does this mean HKO issues signals too early?

No. The lead time demonstrates appropriate forecast-based early warning. Our findings validate that HKO follows published standards. Timing differences are expected and beneficial for public safety.

❓ What about Tapah showing zero detection?

Some typhoons produce localized or offshore gales that don't meet our lowland multi-station criteria, but still justify Signal 8 based on exposure, high-ground winds, and forecast conditions.

❓ Can I use this to predict the next typhoon?

No. This is retrospective validation only, not a forecasting tool. HKO's operational forecasts incorporate far more data (radar, satellite, models) than our simple observation-based algorithm.

❓ Where does the data come from?

All wind data comes from HKO's public archives of 10-minute mean wind speeds at reference stations. We process this data using our validation algorithm. Source code and raw data are openly available.

❓ Is this official?

No. This is an independent citizen analysis for transparency and education. It is not endorsed by or affiliated with HKO. Our goal is validation and public understanding, not criticism.
